Open-problems roundtables

The day closes with ten topic-anchored tables, each led by an invited speaker, panelist, or program-committee member around a provocation question drawn from the pillars — roughly 50 minutes of discussion, then a 2-minute report-out per table into a shared, public notes document.

R1
Process reward models · Weak-to-strong generalization · Scalable oversight

When a weaker verifier supervises a stronger generator, under what conditions does process-level supervision hold, and how do we detect silent degradation?

R2
Recursive self-improvement · Non-stationary optimization · Evaluation-harness stability

If an agent rewrites the objective it optimizes against, what stops recursive self-improvement from collapsing into instability?

R3
Automated pipeline design · On-policy distillation

Which parts of the research pipeline can agents own end-to-end, and where does human oversight remain irreducible?

R4
Automated algorithm discovery · LLM-guided program synthesis · Evolutionary & population-based search

After Claudini and FunSearch, what distinguishes genuine algorithmic discovery from sophisticated retrieval?

R5
Multi-agent environment design · Coordination-failure analysis

Given the “curse of coordination,” what environment designs elicit rather than mask multi-agent coordination failures?

R6
Long-horizon task evaluation · Reward-hacking detection

What do agents actually do over long horizons — degrade, give up, or game the reward — and how do we observe it?

R7
Human–agent co-evolution

As agents reshape the tools and data humans use, how do we study co-evolution without our benchmarks drifting out from under us?

R8
Agent security · Certified adversarial robustness

Where is the security boundary between a research agent and the systems it touches, and can any guarantees survive a self-modifying adversary?

R9
Benchmark design for research agents · Validity of agent-generated claims

When an agent designs and runs its own experiments, what makes the resulting benchmark or claim methodologically sound?

R10
Reproducibility in agent-generated experiments

If the experimenter is a model, what provenance must it emit for its results to be independently reproducible?
