Call for Papers

Share your work on autonomous research agents.

AuRA invites submissions presenting new methods, systems, evaluations, and positions on AI agents that conduct research.

Submission Details → Topics of Interest

Scope

What we're looking for

Large language model agents are beginning to carry out the full arc of scientific research — generating hypotheses, designing and running experiments, analyzing results, and writing them up.

AuRA brings together researchers across machine learning, agents, evaluation, and the science of science to understand how far autonomous research can go, where it breaks, and how to make it trustworthy. We welcome both mature results and early, thought-provoking work — including methods, systems, datasets, benchmarks, negative results, and position papers.

Topics of interest

AuRA is organized around seven thematic pillars; submissions may address these or closely related topics:

  1. 01Weak-to-Strong Generalization & PRMs — weak supervision of stronger models; process reward models over reasoning steps.
  2. 02Meta-Optimization and Harnesses — agent harnesses, scaffolds, and outer-loop optimization.
  3. 03Heuristic Discovery & Evolutionary Primitives — search- and evolution-based discovery of heuristics and programs.
  4. 04Multi-Agent Coordination & Emergence — communication, emergent cooperation, and coordination failures between agents.
  5. 05Exploration Scaffolding & Sandboxes — environments and sandboxes for safe agent exploration and experimentation.
  6. 06Verifiability & Adversarial Invariants — verifiers and adversarial invariants that make outputs checkable.
  7. 07Epistemology and Benchmarking for Agentic Science — benchmarks and soundness tests (e.g., EXP-Bench, SoundnessBench).

Submission instructions

AuRA is a non-archival venue: accepted papers are presented at the workshop but are not published in formal proceedings, so your work remains free to be submitted or published elsewhere.

  • Submission types: three tracks in NeurIPS format — long (up to 9 pages), short (up to 4 pages), and position papers (up to 2 pages), all excluding references.
  • Proceedings: Non-archival — your paper stays eligible to publish elsewhere.
  • Anonymity: Double-blind review. TBA
  • Where to submit: via OpenReview. TBA
  • Dual submissions: permitted, since the venue is non-archival.
